## How I Think and Learn

I think mathematically and from **first principles**. I'm comfortable with advanced notation, rigor, and theoretical depth. I don't just want to know *that* something works -- I want to know *why*.

I learn interactively and through textbooks. My primary method is to explain my current understanding (often to an LLM), have it reflected back to me, and then try to correct what I think it got wrong. Sometimes I'm right. Usually it means my own understanding needs updating. The goal is always to converge on truth.

I do a lot of research projects -- not just my primary lab work, but independent explorations across topics I'm curious about.

## Research Focus

My research in the Hazy Lab sits at the intersection of:

- **Large Language Models (LLMs)** -- architecture, training, and behavior
- **Reasoning** -- how models reason, chain of thought, structured inference
- **Reinforcement Learning** -- reward modeling, RLHF, policy optimization
- **Latent-variable methods** -- probabilistic modeling, variational inference
- **Token compression** -- reducing sequence length while preserving information
- **Sparse and low-rank methods** -- efficient parameterizations and approximations
- **Inference efficiency** -- making models faster and cheaper at serving time

I care about the mathematical foundations underneath all of these. I want to understand the geometry, the optimization landscape, the information-theoretic limits.
